Piece 001 · Poem, 1978

Still I Rise

Maya Angelou

"But still, like dust, I'll rise."

Angelou answers centuries of being written down and written off with pure confidence. She lists everything the world did to keep her there, then rises anyway, in rhythm, like the outcome was never in question. No pleading, no permission. It reads like a dare, and she wins.

Who made this

Hi, I'm Emmanuel Adusi.

There's a double m in my name, Emmanuel, and the title carries the same signature: wearefeminismm. That's not a coincidence. It's my mark, tucked into the name of the whole thing.

I started this because I wanted to teach these ideas in a way nobody would find boring. Every time I've learned about topics like this, I noticed the same thing: it's easy to get overwhelmed, easy to start tuning out, and honestly, a lot of it can just feel boring. So wearefeminismm was born: the same ideas, told in a way that actually holds your attention.

When students were asked, over 44% said they find learning about feminism boring → That number is the whole point. If it comes out lower for the people who spend time here, the site is working, and we're doing great.

This fall I'm heading to the University of Waterloo for Honours Mathematics, and I want to become a quantitative analyst.
