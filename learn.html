So… what is feminism?

One word, three lenses.

It isn't one flat idea. Look through three lenses and the whole picture snaps into focus.

gender

White feminism

Sees one axis: gender. Fights for women, full stop.

not equal weight

Black feminism

Names what the first lens misses: not every woman carries the same weight.

all at once

Intersectionality

The knot that ties it back together: race, gender, class, all at once, under one movement.

✦

The waves of feminism

It didn't arrive all at once. It came in waves.

01
01

1800–1900

The first wave

The right to exist on paper.

  • Won the right to vote
  • Gained legal rights, like owning property
  • Mostly white, upper-class women
02

1960–1980

The second wave

The personal becomes political.

  • Workplace & reproductive rights, education, sexuality
  • Challenged gender roles, like women as housewives
  • Still mainly white, upper-class women
03

1990–2000

The third wave

Room for every kind of woman.

  • Far more diverse: queer & non-binary voices
  • Celebrated the differences between women
  • Intersectionality moved to the center
  • No single goal: deliberately many
04

2010–now

The fourth wave

Loud, online, and for everyone.

  • Fights harassment, assault & online abuse
  • Trans rights, non-binary rights, global identities
  • Spread through social media
  • Bold. Unapologetic. Inclusive.
